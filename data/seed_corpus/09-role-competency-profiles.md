# Role Competency Profiles

These profiles define the competencies a person needs for each role. A readiness system maps a candidate's current skills against the required set and scores the gap.

## Network Engineer
Core competencies: IP addressing and subnetting (VLSM, CIDR), switching and VLANs (802.1Q trunking, STP), routing (static and OSPF), NAT and PAT, ACLs, network redundancy (HSRP), and traffic analysis with Wireshark. Should design and troubleshoot enterprise routing and switching, and document an addressing plan. Foundational tools: Cisco IOS, packet capture.

## Network Security Engineer
Builds on the Network Engineer base and adds: firewall policy and segmentation, defense in depth, vulnerability assessment, intrusion detection and prevention, secure remote access (VPN, hardened SSH), and applying frameworks such as NIST CSF. Should reason about exposure and attack paths, not just connectivity, and translate risk into firewall and segmentation changes.

## SOC Analyst
Core competencies: log analysis and SIEM, intrusion detection (signature and anomaly), the MITRE ATT&CK framework, incident response lifecycle, threat intelligence, and basic scripting for triage. Should triage alerts, separate true positives from noise, map activity to ATT&CK, and follow the incident response process. Less focused on building networks, more on detecting and responding to attacks against them.

Each competency can be assessed at levels: none, aware, working, proficient. Readiness for a role is the weighted coverage of its required competencies at the working level or above.
